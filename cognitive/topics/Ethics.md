Two fields of AI/Robotics and Ethics

**Ai Ethics (responsible AI)**
Set of rules for robots, e.g. should we allow robots to kill, should they be social
We decide these.
Ai ethics is a set of values principles and techniques that employ widely accepted standards of right and wrong to guide moral conduct in development and use of AI technologies.
1. Deontological ethics (we assume we know absolute right and wrong)
	1. Universal rules of right and wrong, Kant.
	2. Universally applicable cross culturally
	3. Not what happens in the real world
2. Utilitarianism
	1. Greatest possible good for greatest number of people
	2. Compute goodness score of action
	3. Need way to value, game theory etc can help model.
	4. Used somewhat
3. Virtue ethics
	1. Most common application
	2. No right and wrong for majority
	3. Let individuals decide with judgement
		1. Special knowledge and roles of people
		2. Ask wise people to tell us what is right and wrong
	4. related to machine ethics

**Machine Ethics**
Robot decides its own law and ethical judgement with its own algorithm
e.g. save small child or old person automatically.
An ethical machine is guided by its own intrinsic ethical rules to decide how to act in a given situation
Take ethical cases, provide to a machine learning algorithm.
4 levels of machine
- ethical impact agents: machine that can be evaluated for ethical consequences
- Implicit ethical agents: machines designed to avoid unethical outcomes
- Explicit ethical agents: machines that can reason about ethics
- Full ethical agents: machines that can make explicit moral judgements and justify them
