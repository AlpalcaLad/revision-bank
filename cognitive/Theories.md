**Genetic Epistemology Theory**
Made by Piaget
Shows how you represent and see the world in a qualitative way
Sensorimotor stage - physical reactions of properties e.g. what does a child think water does (birth to 2 years)
Preoperational stage - representation of objects, e.g. learn words to describe water, meaning of words (2 to 7 years)
Concrete operational stage - experimental stage, think logically about events e.g. are 2 cups of water the same, mix the water (7 to 11 years)
Formal operational stage - more abstract, e.g. think more philosophically (12 and up)

**Wiener's Cybernetics theory**
Principle of self-regulating (homeostasis)
need to find an equilibrium that makes robot stable (e.g. happy for a human)
e.g. if a robot's battery is down and needs to recharge, finds stage where you can charge to try to bring back to balance.

**Grey Walter's Tortoises theory**
Created small robots with simple neural-like circuits and sensory feedback to mimic complex behaviour that looks intelligent
e.g. made robots that mimic Pavlov's dog experiments
Started with Machine Speculatric later made machine docilis

**Braitenburg's vehicles**
Created images of vehicles with different wirings to mimic human behaviours, along with motors
- sensor from left to the right and vice versa, allows for basic movememt
- sensor connected to a motor on the same side, allows for quick movements which can mimic fear
- sensor connected to both motors, allows for sharp turns which can mimic attacking
